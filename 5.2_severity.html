AI-Generated Summary of Expert Comments: Main harms identified include job losses potentially reaching $10 million to $1 billion, documented loss of lives from intoxications, drug misuse, self-medication, and suicides linked to emotional dependency on AI assistants particularly among youth, and gradual disempowerment through cheap attention capture, sticky defaults, and value lock-in. Under Business as Usual, experts expect substantial to severe harm as agency erosion becomes structurally embedded with automation shaping opinions, employment, and behavior without transparency, already occurring across industries. Under Pragmatic Mitigations, human-in-command governance, explainability, consent-based interaction, and user education shift risk toward minor and substantial levels. However, experts note a precarious balance between overuse and realizing benefits, with several warning that mitigations can counterintuitively increase tail risks by creating false security around "solved" problems, that pragmatic measures leave large gaps when users delegate poorly due to low AI literacy, and that systemic failure modes like gradual disempowerment are rarely addressed by cost-effective controls.
