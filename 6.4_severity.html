AI-Generated Summary of Expert Comments: Experts see race dynamics as a key driver of risk, with companies prioritizing speed over safety. Under Business as Usual, substantial to severe harm is expected as competition drives under-regulated releases and reduced safety diligence. One expert draws parallels to auto and chemical industries, noting "winner-take-all dynamics" create even stronger pressures. Specific risks include autonomous weapons malfunctions, AI-generated election disinformation, and potential AI-bio events. Under Pragmatic Mitigations, experts see moderate improvement potential. One suggests industry self-regulation through mutual insurance and safety standards could prevent "AI Three Mile Island" scenarios, noting it's in developers' interests to avoid regulatory backlash. Others propose transparency commitments, coordinated testing, and information sharing. However, skepticism remains - one notes "competitive pressures may still create negative externalities" even with pragmatic policies. International coordination is seen as particularly uncertain, with government 'nudges' like incident sharing potentially useful domestically but less clear effects globally.

Small nudges from the government can also have outsized impact in prompting these sorts of collaborations (e.g. the gov could facilitate greater incident reporting and information sharing between competing developers or insurers). I'm less certain about the situation at the international level."
