Reasoning

AI-Generated Summary of Expert Comments: Experts view multi-agent systems as complexity multipliers that compound rather than simply add risk. Under Business as Usual, substantial to severe harm is expected from coordination failures, feedback loops, and emergent behaviors. Key concerns include cascading failures across interconnected systems (finance, logistics, IT), market manipulation, and the development of inscrutable agent communications. Most see this as a catalyst for other risks rather than a standalone threat. Under Pragmatic Mitigations, proposed measures include rate-limiting, identity attestation, circuit breakers, and multi-agent red teaming. These shift probability toward minor outcomes by catching failures earlier, but experts note fundamental limitations - one states mitigations make agents "safer to interact with but not predictable," another notes "very few plausible mitigations" will prevent moderate failures.
