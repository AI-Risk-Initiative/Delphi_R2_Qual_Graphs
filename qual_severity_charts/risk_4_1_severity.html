Under Pragmatic Mitigations including transparency standards, digital provenance, watermarking, verified identity, rate limits, and cross-platform takedown agreements, views diverge on effectiveness, with some experts seeing meaningful shifts toward lower harm through episodic rather than systemic misuse while others express skepticism about what pragmatic measures could achieve given poor societal response to weaker versions of this problem and governments' unwillingness to regulate themselves when AI provides battlefield advantages. Catastrophic outcomes remain possible where such systems destabilize social cohesion or enable authoritarian control. With pragmatic mitigations, including transparency standards, digital provenance, AI watermarking, algorithmic accountability, and rights-based governance, these risks decline meaningfully.
